Rich in potassium (600mg/cup), sodium, magnesium, calcium, and natural sugars. The Philippines' biggest recovery advantage.\n\nPrep: Drink fresh from the coconut (best) or buy bottled with no added sugar\n\nMalunggay (Moringa oleifera)\n\n₱10-20/bundle (fresh); ₱50-80/sachet (dried/powder)\n\nCalled \"miracle malunggay\" in the Philippines — a nutritional powerhouse. Leaves contain 2x the protein of yogurt, 4x the calcium of milk, and 7x the vitamin C of oranges. Excellent for recovery nutrition.\n\nPrep: Add fresh leaves to tinola, mongo, or any soup. Dried powder can be added to smoothies, rice, or tea.\n\nLuyang Dilaw (Turmeric Root)\n\n₱20-50/fresh root bundle; ₱400-600/month (supplement form)\n\nThe local cousin of turmeric, widely available in Philippine markets. Contains curcumin with anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties. A key ingredient in Filipino traditional medicine for various ailments.\n\nPrep: Boil fresh slices as tea (salabat-style). Add to rice, soups, and curries. For supplements, take with black pepper (piperine) for 2000% absorption boost.\n\nFresh Fish (Bangus, Galunggong, Sardines)\n\nGalunggong: ₱60-120/kg; Sardines (canned): ₱15-25/can; Bangus: ₱120-180/kg\n\nThe Philippines is surrounded by seas — fish is the most affordable and culturally integrated omega-3 source. Galunggong (round scad) is the \"national fish\" and costs ₱60-120/kg. Sardines in cans are even cheaper.\n\nPrep: Paksiw (vinegar stew), sinigang (sour soup), grilled (inihaw), or fried. Canned sardines with tomato sauce are a quick omega-3 source.\n\nBanana (Saba and Lacatan)\n\n₱5-15/piece (saba); ₱30-50/hand (lacatan)\n\nBananas are the cheapest and most accessible recovery food in the Philippines. They provide vitamin B6 (essential for serotonin/dopamine synthesis), potassium (electrolytes), and tryptophan (serotonin precursor).\n\nPrep: Eat ripe for maximum tryptophan and sugar (energy). Saba can be boiled (nilagang saba), fried (maruya/banana cue), or used in smoothies.\n\nGarlic (Bawang)\n\n₱80-120/kg; homegrown is free\n\nPhilippine cuisine uses garlic extensively. Allicin (formed when garlic is crushed) is a powerful antioxidant that boosts glutathione production — the body's master antioxidant depleted during substance use.\n\nPrep: Crush or mince and let sit 10 minutes before cooking (activates allicin). Add generously to all savory dishes. Eat 2-3 raw cloves daily for maximum benefit.\n\nAtchara (Pickled Papaya)\n\n₱30-60/small container; homemade: ₱20-30\n\nThe Philippines' beloved side dish is actually a fermented food! The fermentation process produces beneficial bacteria (lactobacilli) that support the gut-brain axis. Papaya also contains papain, a digestive enzyme.\n\nPrep: Eat as side dish with any meal. Homemade atchara (using raw papaya, vinegar, garlic, ginger) is best — commercial versions may have too much sugar.\n\nCamote (Sweet Potato)\n\n₱30-50/kg\n\nCamote is a complex carbohydrate that provides sustained energy and serves as a natural prebiotic. The fiber feeds beneficial gut bacteria. Beta-carotene supports immune function and brain health.\n\nPrep: Boiled (nilagang camote), baked, or as camote cue (snack). Purple camote has additional anthocyanin antioxidants.\n\nTofu (Tokwa)\n\n₱30-50/block (300g)\n\nTofu is a complete protein containing all essential amino acids including tyrosine. It's the cheapest protein source in the Philippines and is widely used in both Filipino and Chinese-Filipino cuisine.\n\nPrep: Tokwa’t baboy (tofu and pork), stir-fried with vegetables, in miso soup, or added to sinigang. Firm tofu has more protein.\n\nAmpalaya (Bitter Melon)\n\n₱20-40/piece\n\nWhile better known for blood sugar control, ampalaya has documented cardiovascular benefits including blood pressure regulation and antioxidant properties. A traditional Filipino medicinal food.\n\nPrep: Pinakbet (Ilocano vegetable stew), sautéed with egg, or as juice (if you can handle the bitterness). Soaking in salt water reduces bitterness.\n\nGreen Tea (from local sources)\n\n₱100-300/box (20-50 bags); Loose leaf: ₱300-600/100g\n\nGreen tea contains EGCG (the neuroprotective compound) and L-theanine (a calming amino acid). While not traditionally Filipino, quality green tea is now widely available and affordable.\n\nPrep: Steep 3-5 minutes at 80°C (not boiling). Drink 2-3 cups daily. Combine with 5-HTP for synergistic neuroprotection. Best taken between meals.\n\nLagundi Leaves\n\n₱20-50/bundle (fresh); ₱100-200 (capsules/tablets)\n\nLagundi is a DOH-recognized traditional medicinal plant in the Philippines. While primarily known for respiratory conditions, it has mild sedative and anti-anxiety properties that can support recovery sleep.\n\nPrep: Boil 4-5 fresh leaves in 2 cups water for 10 minutes. Drink as tea 30 minutes before bed. Available as Ascof Lagundi tablets at Mercury Drug."
